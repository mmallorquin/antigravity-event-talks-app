SUMMARY OF NEWS — June 17, 2025
=================================

The dominant story today is the escalating Israel-Iran military conflict.
Israel struck multiple Iranian cities — including Tehran, Isfahan, and Tabriz —
killing senior military commander Major General Ali Shadmani. Iran retaliated
with strikes on northern Israel and Tel Aviv. The IAEA confirmed damage to
enrichment halls at Iran's Natanz nuclear facility, raising proliferation
concerns. Diplomatically, the U.S. called for Iran's unconditional surrender,
while Iran accused the G7 of bias toward Israel.

In Gaza, over 50 Palestinians were killed near aid distribution centers in the
south, prompting widespread international condemnation.

On the global stage, the China-Central Asia Summit commenced in Astana, and
President Trump left the G7 Summit early.

In data privacy news, 23andMe was fined over CAD $4.2 million by British
authorities for a 2023 data breach that exposed millions of users' genetic
information.

KEY THEMES:
  1. Middle East conflict escalation (Israel-Iran war, Gaza crisis)
  2. Geopolitical shifts (G7, China-Central Asia relations)
  3. Data privacy enforcement (23andMe penalty)
